The Narrative

Built on Craft.
Backed by Strategy.

I started my career at ELB Learning — building bespoke, award-winning courseware for Fortune 500 clients and earning a Brandon Hall Award for Excellence in Learning for best use of games and simulation. That foundation in craft has never left me. But over 15 years, the work has grown from building courses to building systems, and from designing learning to leading organizations through change.

At Twitch, I owned engineering education end-to-end for a 500+ person globally distributed organization — treating it as an internal business unit with a budget, a roadmap, KPIs, and a direct line to engineering leadership. I built the program from scratch, embedded development into team workflows rather than running it as a separate function, and reduced new-hire time-to-first-contribution to under 30 days. At Terminix, I led the Workday Learn LMS transformation for a 10,000+ employee enterprise and designed capability programs for 5,000+ frontline employees across field operations, call centers, and corporate functions.

More recently I've been advising federal agencies and PE-backed enterprises on organizational diagnostics, AI-enabled ways of working, and capability roadmaps that connect learning to business performance — not just training completion.

The thread across all of it: I close the gap between 'training completed' and 'person productive.' That gap is almost always a design and systems problem. I fix it by embedding development into the flow of work, building leaders who can build their own teams, and treating L&D as an organizational capability rather than a department function.

On the AI front — I don't just talk about it. I built Undrift (undrift.io) because I kept hitting the same facilitation problem and decided to fix it rather than work around it. That's how I approach most things. I've also led AI adoption programs for federal and enterprise workforces, helping organizations move from individual tool use to genuine capability at scale.
